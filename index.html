Up to this point, we've focused on lower-level math and pre-algebra. We've primarily worked with regular numbers like 3, 0, or -7, along with fractions and decimals.

Now, in algebra, we're introducing variables. A variable is a symbol for a number that we don't know yet. Commonly, we use "X" or "Y" to represent variables, but technically, we can use any letter.

Consider the equation: 3 + 2 = 5. In this equation, 3, 2, and 5 are all constants, regular numbers. If we replace the 3 with the variable "X," we get: X + 2 = 5. It's the same equation, but now with a variable.

By matching these up, we can deduce that X must equal 3. Why? Because what do we add to 2 to get 5? The value is 3. This is the essence of solving equations.

Key Terms

  • Variable: A symbol for a number we don't know yet.
  • Constant: Regular numbers like 3 or -7.
  • Equation: A mathematical statement with an equal sign, indicating equivalence between the left and right sides.
  • Coefficient: A number (or sometimes a letter) in front of a variable, indicating multiplication.
  • Expression: A mathematical phrase consisting of terms.
  • Term: Any single number, variable, or the product of a number and variable.

For instance, in the equation 3X - 10 = 8, "3" is the coefficient, "X" is the variable, "3X" and "10" are terms, and the entire expression is an equation.

Understanding these concepts is crucial as you dive deeper into algebra. Keep practicing, and don't hesitate to ask if you have any questions!
